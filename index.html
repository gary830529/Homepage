About me

Welcome to my webpage. My name is Hsiang-Yang (Gary) Shyu, and I have the privilege of working as a Ph.D. candidate under the mentorship of Dr. Daniel Yeh in his Membrane Biotechnology Lab in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at the University of South Florida.

Our research team is at the cutting edge of engineering wastewater treatment technologies specifically designed for non-sewered sanitation. The NEWgenerator™ stands as our most notable contribution, a revolutionary off-grid treatment system that not only treats wastewater but also repurposes it, transforming waste into valuable resources like nutrients, energy, and water.

In my crucial role in this transformative project, I am responsible for advancing membrane-based separation technologies and innovating advanced adsorbents for nutrient recycling. My work includes the meticulous analysis of data collected from a field test system in South Africa, with the goal of developing dynamic models to optimize water, nutrient, and energy cycles within the system. Further enriching this work, I have embraced the field of machine learning (ML), applying it to enhance our wastewater treatment capabilities. I have contributed to the development of a real-time remote monitoring system for our system, utilizing ML algorithms to create soft sensors that predict key water quality parameters. This innovation provides invaluable real-time insights, significantly boosting the operational efficiency of the treatment system.

Before delving into my Ph.D. studies, I completed my Master’s degree in Environmental Engineering right here at the University of South Florida. During that period, my research centered on applying a flotation microalgae bioreactor within aquaponic systems and wastewater treatment plants.
