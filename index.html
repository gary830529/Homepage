About Me

My name is Hsiang-Yang (Gary) Shyu and I am a postdoctoral research scholar working with Dr. Daniel Yeh in the Membrane Biotechnology Lab at the University of South Florida.

Our research focuses on advanced wastewater treatment for non-sewered sanitation. I lead efforts within the NEWgenerator™ project—an innovative system that transforms wastewater into clean water, nutrients, and energy.

My expertise involves membrane-based separation, nutrient recycling adsorbents, and the integration of machine learning to develop soft sensors for real-time water quality monitoring.

I earned my Ph.D. in Civil and Environmental Engineering from USF in 2024. I am honored to be the 14th doctoral graduate from the Membrane Biotechnology Lab!
